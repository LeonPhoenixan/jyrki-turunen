UX Design & Research  ·  Visma 2020–Present
UX — Building a Practice from Scratch

Joined the M2 product team in 2020 as its first ever UX resource, inheriting a product with 20+ years of history and no prior UX investment. With the product owner's support and considerable freedom — and responsibility — the first task was to find out what users actually thought.

The first research cycle (2020) involved a questionnaire reaching 200+ respondents, 12 user interviews, a competitor analysis, and a four-person heuristic evaluation of a core product flow. The resulting data was synthesised into empathy maps for all interviewees, four personas, and journey maps built in collaborative sessions with development team members and an external designer. All findings were presented to the wider product team and made available as living documents to inform design priorities.

The years between cycles were spent translating research into designs — wireframing first in Adobe XD, later Figma — while managing the full UX workload of the product solo. In 2022, a UX trainee position was championed and secured, enabling a second research cycle: 300+ survey responses, ~10 interviews, and for the first time observed task completion sessions and wireframe testing with real users. Results were compared against 2020 data, providing the first longitudinal view of how the product and its users had evolved. Around the same time the first accessibility audit of the browser product was completed, and growing investment in the mobile app added further scope to the work.

By end of 2023, access to Amplitude enabled a new layer of insight. A UX metrics dashboard was built from scratch, and from 2024 onwards a monthly UX metrics report has been produced and reviewed with the product owner and product manager in bi-weekly meetings — bringing quantitative usage data into the conversation alongside the qualitative research.

In 2025, a third research cycle was initiated with a new trainee — again championed and recruited from start to finish, including personally reviewing all 200+ applications. This cycle introduced a user panel: a repository of willing participants available for ongoing tests and prototype feedback, addressing a long-standing gap in the team's ability to get fast user input. Data from all three cycles now provides a multi-year longitudinal picture of user needs and product evolution. The 2025 trainee has since remained with the team and is being actively mentored.

Accessibility  ·  Visma 2020–Present
Accessibility Education & Advocacy

A self-initiated accessibility programme built from scratch together with a colleague. We created a comprehensive training package covering accessibility fundamentals and delivered it across all product teams in the organisation — roughly 12–15 three-hour sessions in both Finnish and English, receiving strong feedback throughout. Alongside the training, we brought in an external auditor to review our products and produce accessibility reports against legal requirements. We also founded an accessibility champions movement, embedding 1–2 advocates in each product team to keep accessibility part of the development conversation going forward. To further deepen the expertise behind this work, I completed the IxDF course "Accessibility: How to Design for All" in September 2025, graduating in the top 10% of the class.

UI Design  ·  Visma 2020–Present
UI Design — Wireframes & Layouts

A substantial body of UI work spanning the full time at Visma — creating new screens and flows, modifying existing ones, and refreshing legacy designs as functionality migrates from older systems to the current product. Work covers both browser and mobile environments and requires careful adherence to multiple coexisting design systems, demanding precision and a strong eye for consistency across a complex product landscape. UI design is the baseline of this profession and, increasingly, an area where AI will take on the heavy lifting — with designers verifying, refining and owning the last 10–20% rather than starting from a blank canvas. The experience here spans a decade — and if a prospective employer wants to see the work in action, I'm happy to put together an example flow on whatever brief they have in mind.

UX Research  ·  Bilot 2018–2019
Housing_Company Software — UX Research

A qualitative UX research project combining observation sessions and user interviews to evaluate an enterprise software product used by a housing company client. Conducted with end users across multiple office locations, the study mapped daily usage patterns, pain points, and mental models. Findings were analysed using affinity mapping and resulted in a prioritised list of development recommendations covering onboarding, in-app guidance, information architecture, loading feedback, and terminology.

Facilitation  ·  Bilot & Visma 2017–Present
Lego Scrum Workshops

Designed and facilitated Scrum training workshops using Lego as the hands-on medium, run at both Bilot and Visma. Participants — typically a mix of developers, designers and other staff — were cast as the best and brightest selected for a Mars colony project, tasked with building a functioning city using Scrum methodology in real time. Sessions ran 2–3 hours: the first 30–60 minutes covering Scrum fundamentals, the remainder putting it into practice through timed sprints with planning, development and review phases. One facilitator ran the session while another played the project manager role.
